Text Editor

Learn Enough Text Editor to Be Dangerous, Lesson the Second

Ahh text editors, I'm reliably informed there are many but my experience is extremely limited to two. A very very brief skim of the top of the soup skin of Vim *shudders*, and an enjoyable sip of the comforting tea that is Atom.
So what do I know?

  • Just enough Vim so that I can know what it is and how to get the hell out ESC:q!, search /, write and quit :wq etc.
  • Tasty, tasty atom with it’s lovely colours and whatnot (syntax highlighting)
  • Keyboard commands/ shortcuts
  • Find and replace
  • Autocomplete/ tab triggers
  • Source code
  • Executable script
  • Shell scripts (automating away common tasks)
  • Editing things
  • Customisation
  • Interface
  • Add-on dooberry’s (dat mini map)
  • THOU SHALT NOT HAVE OVER 80 COLUMNS

I cannot stress enough the importance of knowing when to quit (Vim). I have it writ large on a neon pink post-it, just in case my brain forgets and the Web melts.
ESC:q!

So same here as with the command line, I am painfully aware that I've barely scratched the surface of what these can do. One of my favourite things is being able to generate skeletons with like, 2 key strokes. But, there are probably THOUSANDS of these little short cuts. That takes time, comfortability with the editor, and a good Google around probably.
The other main thing I've taken away from this:
FOR THE LOVE OF GOD CLOSE YOUR GODDAMN TAGS.
Even while writing this up, I've fallen prey to forgetting to close </li> this little bugger.
CONSTANT VIGILANCE.
